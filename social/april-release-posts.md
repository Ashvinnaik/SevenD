### Option A — Practitioner Angle

**SevenD April Release: What a year of AI-assisted development taught us about project structure**

I've spent the last 12 months building 34 projects with AI coding agents — from weekend prototypes to enterprise-grade platforms. Along the way, I tracked everything: 5,775+ AI interactions, 425 commits, 57 governance rules.

One pattern kept emerging: projects fail not because the AI is bad, but because the structure around it is missing.

The biggest lesson came in December. We hit our highest velocity ever — 325 commits in a single month. It was also our worst month for quality. Fix-to-build ratio of 1.8:1 meant we were spending nearly twice as much time fixing as building.

The solution wasn't better prompts. It was better governance.

Today I'm releasing the April update to SevenD, an open-source framework for structuring AI-assisted development. Here's what changed:

**Sprint-based phases** — Every phase (Discovery through Deployment) now runs its own sprints at its own cadence. Real projects aren't linear. The structure shouldn't be either.

**Zero-bug sprint close** — A sprint can only close when its Bug section hits zero. This single rule dropped our fix-to-build ratio from 1.8:1 to 0.7:1.

**Reference directory** — Stable documents (architecture, component registry, interface contracts, decision log) live separately from active sprint work. Your AI agent reads these once and knows the full system context.

**STATUS.md** — One file that answers "where are we right now?" at a glance.

SevenD has 4 levels — from 2 files for a weekend project to full GitHub-native with Actions and PR gates. It also ships as a Claude skill that walks you through setup.

The framework wasn't designed in advance. It was extracted from the patterns that actually worked across those 34 projects. The full research is published as well.

Repo: https://github.com/Ashvinnaik/SevenD

Research: https://powerprompts-website-prod.s3.us-east-1.amazonaws.com/assets/AI-Human+Interactions+Report.pdf

If you're building with AI agents and feel like you're fighting the tool more than shipping with it — structure is the missing piece.
